## Discussion Points
1. **Single-residue change, systemic impact:** The computational evidence demonstrates how a solitary amino-acid change cascades into macroscopic pathology, highlighting the importance of precise sequence work.
2. **Reproducibility as a teaching tool:** Each step regenerates deterministic outputs, making it easier to critique or extend the workflow (Wilkinson et al. 2016 FAIR principles).
3. **Interaction data is context, not proof:** BioGRID/IntAct counts vary; by isolating them in a supporting note, the main narrative stays grounded in direct sequence/structure evidence.
